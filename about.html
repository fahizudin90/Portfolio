How I got here,
and how I work.

Three years in as the sole UI/UX designer on my team, still looking for better ways to make dense workflows make sense.

I'm the sole UI/UX designer on a B2B accounting platform used by thousands of businesses. I turn accounting workflows and business requirements into user journeys, wireframes, prototypes, and shipped UI, with a focus on consistency across dense, data-heavy screens.

I usually define the workflow and interaction logic in Figma before formal specs exist. That gives Business Analysts a concrete design to convert into requirements, and gives Engineering something real to build against instead of a written brief alone.

I stay close to development and QA after handoff too, checking that edge cases and error states actually made it into the build, not just the happy path.
